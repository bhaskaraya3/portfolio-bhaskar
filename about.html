About Me

I work with data to turn messy numbers into clear, actionable insights. I specialize in building interactive dashboards in Power BI and analyzing data using SQL, Python, and Excel to support better decision-making.

I regularly work on data analysis projects, dashboards, and machine learning concepts, and I share my work publicly to learn faster, improve continuously, and build credibility.

I am open to internships, entry-level roles, and collaborations in data analytics and related fields.

My Journey into Data

My journey into data began with programming in school, where Python was my first language. Over time, my interest shifted from writing code to understanding how businesses operate through data across domains like fintech, edtech, and sales.

As I grew and explored different career paths within programming, I became particularly interested in data and how organizations rely on it to function. Looking at datasets helped me understand how decisions are made and outcomes are driven by numbers rather than assumptions.

This exposure shifted my mindset. I didn’t just want to write code; I wanted to understand what the data was saying and turn raw numbers into meaningful visuals and insights.

How I Approach Data

I start by understanding the structure of the dataset, reviewing columns and possible relationships. I then clean with intent—handling missing values, duplicates, and inconsistencies.

Once clean, I use SQL to explore trends, correlations, and business context, and Python to visualize insights clearly and effectively.

Skills & Current Focus

I am confident with advanced SQL, Power BI, and Python libraries such as Pandas, NumPy, and Matplotlib. I focus on building dashboards and analyses that translate data into decisions.

At the same time, I am strengthening my understanding of statistics, DAX, and machine learning fundamentals to go beyond what happened and understand why it happened.

Goals & Direction

The rapid impact of artificial intelligence on today’s world and its long-term potential strongly motivates me. Beyond the obvious growth opportunities in this space, I am deeply curious about how intelligent systems learn from data and how those insights can scale decision-making across industries.

Over the next one to two years, my focus is on building a strong professional foundation by working in data-driven roles where I can gain real-world experience, sharpen my analytical thinking, and work closely with business data. I see data analysis as a critical stepping stone — one that builds the intuition, discipline, and context required to grow into an AI/ML engineering role over time.

What I’m Looking For

I am open to a wide range of opportunities, including remote internships, full-time roles, part-time work, freelance projects, and collaborations in data analytics and related fields. I am especially interested in roles where I can work with real data, take ownership of tasks, and continuously improve through hands-on experience.

I value learning-focused and structured environments, including corporate teams where clear processes, accountability, and collaboration are encouraged. Working alongside experienced professionals and contributing meaningfully to shared goals is something I actively look for.
